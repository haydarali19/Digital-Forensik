Challenge Brief

What we need to recover

The evidence bundle contains route screenshots and terminal captures from a drone log analysis session. Our goal is to turn those fragments into a readable investigation page that explains what happened and how the crash location was derived.

Toolkit

What gets used

  • QGroundControl to inspect the mission path
  • `mavlogdump.py` to enumerate telemetry and message types
  • Focused GPS and MSG extraction for the final answer

Mission Overview

The flight path immediately gives us the story arc

Before touching the terminal output, we look at the big picture. The drone launches from the southwest, loops across the Citadella area, crosses the river, and continues toward the city center. This context makes the later crash and coordinate extraction much easier to trust.

Step by Step

Rebuilding the analysis like a proper forensic note

01

Start with the full mission trace

The first screenshot shows the entire route and elevation profile. It tells us the mission is not a random short hop, but a multi-waypoint planned flight with a long return arc.

02

Zoom in on the mission geometry

A tighter crop helps show the spline waypoints, hover times, and the shape of the path around the landmark. That matters because it confirms the mission was intentionally programmed, not flown freehand.

03

Enumerate the available log record types

Once the visual route is understood, the next move is to ask the binary log what record families are available. This is the fastest way to know whether GPS, message, attitude, and mission artifacts exist.

04

Read the human-readable mission messages

The `MSG` records are especially helpful because they narrate the mission. We can see initialization, GPS usage, mission start, and waypoint progress, which turns the opaque log into a timeline.

05

Find the crash event first, then pivot to GPS

A compact `MSG` query reveals the critical line: the simulator reports a ground hit at a descending velocity. Around that same timestamp, the GPS record gives us the exact latitude and longitude we need.

06

Answer the challenge with concise extraction commands

The final terminal snippets boil the solution down into direct outputs: first coordinate pair and a maximum groundspeed value. This is the kind of clean ending that makes a writeup easy to audit.
